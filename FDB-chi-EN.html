FDB Heavy Mode χ Quasiparticle (English, full version)

This page summarizes the heavy mode χ quasiparticle in the FDB model. χ is an internal excitation of the ULW‑EM sector that appears DM‑like but lives only inside the galactic evanescent shell.

1. Positioning and basic picture

1.1 FDB background (intuitive)

  • Space is filled with ultra‑low‑frequency EM waves (ULE‑EMW).
  • Around disks/bulges, steep plasma gradients create evanescent fields.
  • ULE‑EMW + evanescent field + plasma gradients bend light and stellar orbits → “DM‑like gravity.”
    χ is a heavy eigenmode (quasi‑particle) inside this structure.

1.2 What is χ (intuition)

  • Origin: heavy eigenmode inside ULE‑EMW sector
  • As seen from the SM: neutral, no color, mass ~ tens–hundreds GeV, very weak SM coupling → DM‑like.
  • Unlike ΛCDM DM, χ exists only inside the evanescent shell; outside, it dissolves back into ULW‑EM modes.

2. Field description and parameters

2.1 As an effective field

Example (real scalar):

$$ \mathcal{L}_{\text{free}} = \tfrac12(\partial_\mu \chi)(\partial^\mu \chi) - \tfrac12 m_\chi^2 \chi^2 $$

Mass scale (set by 10–100 GeV γ spectrum):

mχ ∼ 50-500 GeV

Compton wavelength:

$$ \lambda_C = \frac{\hbar}{m_\chi c} $$

e.g., mχ = 100 GeV ⇒ λC ∼ 2 × 10−18 m;
mχ = 500 GeV ⇒ λC ∼ 4 × 10−19 m — point‑like astrophysically.

3. Production: ULE‑EMW → χ

Produced only inside the shell where ULE‑EMW, plasma gradients, and evanescent field overlap:

Qχ(r) ∝ |E⃗ULE(r)|2 |∇ne(r)|2

χ / anti‑χ pairs are generated from ULE‑EMW energy; outside the shell they re‑merge into ULW‑EM modes.

4. Stability and annihilation

Annihilation timescale:

$$ \tau_{\text{ann}}\sim \frac{1}{n_\chi\langle\sigma v\rangle} $$

With nχ ∼ 0.1 cm−3, ⟨σv⟩ ∼ 10−26-25 cm3/s:

τann ∼ 1026 s

Universe age  ∼ 4 × 1017 s → cosmologically stable (8–9 orders longer).

5. Gamma‑ray budget

Central GC 10–100 GeV flux:

Jγ ∼ 10−11-10−10 W/m2/sr

Energy leak fraction:

$$ \frac{j_\gamma}{\rho_\chi}\sim10^{-26}\ \mathrm{s^{-1}} $$

Only a tiny fraction of χ energy needs to annihilate to match observed γ.

6. Spatial distribution (“shell” role)

  • Inside shell: χ is a defined mode; number density nχ meaningful.
  • Outside shell: mode dissolves; gravity is set by ULW‑EM field, not χ mass.
    Thus χ is accessory; the main gravitating “mass” is still the FDB field structure.

7. Gamma‑ray morphology

Annihilation channels:

χχ → SM → π0 → γγ

or

$$ \frac{\alpha_{\text{eff}}}{4\Lambda}\,\chi F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu} $$

Intensity:

Iγ(l,b) ∝ ∫ρχ2(r(l,b,s)) ds

→ GC‑centered, quasi‑spherical; dwarfs are faint because Qχ is intrinsically small there.

8. Time evolution (schematic)

$$ \frac{dn_\chi}{dt}=Q_{\text{prod}}-\langle\sigma v\rangle n_\chi^2-\Gamma_{\text{leak}} n_\chi $$

Formation: rises as disks/bulges and gradients form; later, production ≈ annihilation + leak → quasi‑steady today.

9. Summary

  • χ = heavy ULW‑EM eigenmode, WIMP‑scale, neutral, confined to the evanescent shell.
  • Explains GC 10–100 GeV γ excess without requiring cosmic‑mean DM density; dwarfs naturally faint.
  • Gravity in FDB comes from ULW‑EM field geometry; χ is not the primary gravitating mass.
