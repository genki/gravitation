Informing Tensor in FDB Theory (English, full version)

1. Overview

In the FDB picture, gravity is not “curvature of spacetime” but information transport carried solely by the causal wavefront of ultra‑low‑energy EM (ULW‑EM). That carrier field is the informing tensor Iμν.

  • Corresponds to the spin‑2 part of linearized GR (hμνTT)
  • Explicitly includes medium effects of the cosmic web and galactic evanescent layers on ULW‑EM wavefronts

2. Basic idea (two layers)

  1. Informing layer: Motion / inertial bias of matter is broadcast causally by ULW‑EM wavefronts; this is encoded in Iμν.
  2. Potential layer: Static or accumulated parts of Iμν build an “information potential” ΦFDB whose gradient is the observed gravitational potential.

Thus Iμν “tells” the state of sources, reconstituting the role of GR’s waves + weak‑field potential.

3. Definition: building the informing tensor

3.1 Matter tensor and Green function

Stress–energy of matter: Tαβ(mat)(x).
For ULW‑EM (Proca‑type):

(□+μ2)Aμ(x) = Jμ(x)

Solution with retarded Green function:

Aμ(x) = ∫d4x′ Gret(x,x′) Jμ(x′)

Hadamard split:

Gret = Gfront + Gtail

FDB: only the front part carries “gravitational” information. Minkowski, μ → 0:

$$ G_{\text{front}}(x,x') = \frac{1}{2\pi}\,\theta(t-t')\,\delta\!\big((x-x')^2\big) $$

3.2 TT projection and definition

Introduce the transverse–traceless projector PTT (spin‑2, trace‑free). Define

Iμν(x) ≡ κ (PTT)μναβ∫d4x′ Gfront(x,x′) Tαβ(mat)(x′)

  • κ: FDB coupling (maps to effective Geff)
  • PTT: keeps only the spin‑2 transverse traceless part
  • Gfront: causal wavefront; support only on the light cone

Embedded properties:

  • Causality: only light‑cone front contributes.
  • Spin‑2: only two GR‑like polarizations remain.

4. Equations of motion and GR correspondence

4.1 Vacuum

□Iμν ≃ 0,  ∂μIμν = 0,  Iμμ = 0

Same form as the linearized GR wave equation.

4.2 Far zone & quadrupole

With $G_{\text{front}}\sim\frac{1}{4\pi}\frac{\delta(t'-(t-r/c))}{r}$:

$$ I_{\mu\nu} \simeq \frac{\kappa}{4\pi r}(P_{\text{TT}})_{\mu\nu}{}^{\alpha\beta} \int d^3x'\, T^{\text{(mat)}}_{\alpha\beta}\left(t-\frac{r}{c},\mathbf{x}'\right) $$

Mass quadrupole:

$$ Q_{ij} = \int d^3x'\,\rho(t,\mathbf{x}')\left(x'_i x'_j-\tfrac13\delta_{ij}|\mathbf{x}'|^2\right) $$

Far‑field TT part:

$$ I_{ij}^{\text{TT}} \simeq \frac{2G_{\text{eff}}}{c^4 r}\,\frac{d^2 Q_{ij}^{\text{TT}}}{dt^2}\bigg|_{t-r/c} $$

Radiated power (informing radiation):

$$ P = -\frac{G_{\text{eff}}}{5c^5}\Big\langle \dddot{Q}_{ij}\dddot{Q}_{ij} \Big\rangle $$

→ Identical to GR quadrupole formula; binary pulsar decay is reproduced.

5. “Universality of notice” and the information potential

5.1 Universality (distance‑blind front)

Because Gfront lives on the light cone, the “notice” about source motion reaches near and far observers identically (up to light‑travel time). GR‑like universality is preserved.

5.2 Link to the potential ΦFDB

ΦFDB(x) = ∫d3x′ K(x,x′) ρ(x′)

The kernel K includes static parts of Iμν, loop accumulation, reflections:

  • Spherical systems: Ksphere ≈ e−r/λ/r (Newton/Yukawa‑like).
  • Disks/filaments: waveguiding and loops amplify Kdisk, producing flat rotation curves.

6. Boundary conditions: galactic evanescent layer

  • Static ultra‑long ULW‑EM modes are trapped in the disk’s evanescent zone → boost ΦFDB locally.
  • Faster modes transmit as informing radiation → consistent with LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA detections from distant binaries.

Splitting modes this way lets FDB explain disk rotation (static) while allowing GW transparency (dynamic).
